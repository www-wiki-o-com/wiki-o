Composing the Statement
Statements must be composed without implication. For example the statement "Anyone with a brain can see that Trump is a genius" implies that the statement is true. It should instead be composed as "Trump is a genius".

The false statement must be the logical complement and must not make any additional implications. For example the statement "Trump is stupid" is not the compliment of "Trump is a genius", "Trump is not a genius" is the complement.
Links
There are three types of links that can be included in the details section: inline, bib, and bib without text reference. Inline links are surrounded by <> and contain the url and title separated by a space. Bib links are similarly composed with square brackets and bib links without text reference are composed with double square brackets. If bib links are included, then a bibliography will be automatically appended to the text.

Example: A bib link to this site would look like [www.wiki-o.com Wiki-O].
